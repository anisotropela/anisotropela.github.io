I am an astrophysicist ("postdoctoral fellow") at the University of Oslo. My work is mainly numerical/theoretical (as opposed to observational/experimental). That is, I develop computer programs that simulate the physics of the Universe, and use these programs to interpret and predict real observations.

I'm particularly interested in the early Universe, how galaxies formed, and which physical properties governed the galaxies at that time. Why? Because galaxies are enigmatic and immensely beautiful, besides being the building blocks of our Universe. Understanding these guys helps us understand the cosmos around us and our place in it.

More specifically, I use gravito/hydro-dynamical codes to simulate the formation of galaxies, and subsequently simulate the radiative transfer of their emitted light, i.e. how they would actually look if observed from Earth. In essence, by comparing real and synthetic observations, I can learn about the physical parameters of the real ones, e.g. their masses, star formation, gas kinematics, temperatures, dust contents, etc.

More generally, I would like to contribute to expanding our horizon, in one way or another.
